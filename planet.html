    Planetary Embodiment
    Experience the bigger picture

    • Mar'16 - Apr'16 (5 weeks)

    • Group of 4

    • Slide Show

    For this project, we wanted to explore the concept of how best to design an experience for a museum about space. Right now, most museums about space try to convey a lot of large facts and figures that are difficult to understand or relate to. We wanted to create an experience that allowed users to feel more connected to space by having them embody a planet. People feel disconnected from the Earth because they only ever experience a tiny part of it. We want to allow people to have a larger experience outside of themselves, to expand their tiny part of the Earth into a whole planet, and let that experience show them that their problems are not so bad. By allowing them to experience this together, it is our hope that they will feel more connected to each other, and more connected to the universe as well. Astronauts who have traveled to space experience something called the Overview Effect; this basically says that the first time you see the Earth from space, you realize how small and fragile humanity is, and how important it is to cherish it and protect it.


    Introduction

    We started with a basic question- What is the purpose of a space museum? Is it teaching facts or is it to inspire about space? Thats when we realized that space museum have an important job to inspire people. We looked at different ways that it can inspire people like inspire kids about space and inspire everyone to look at bigger picture.


    Process

    The focus in this project was on experience (not the design detail but the experience created by the design) and how to break away from designers fixation. We used creative methods such as experience prototype, pastiche scenarios and critical design to explore different design concepts.


    Exemplars and Ideation

    The ideation process started with search for different kind of exemplars, we looked at different museums and also any different ideas related to space. Then, we analyzed what makes these ideas different/ unique? These elements became the base for ideation and we conducted multiple ideation session building on these concepts. What stood out in these concepts was that 'space/ solar system/ universe' makes us feel small. This feel was missing in most of the space museum around us. That lead us to one basic rule for our space museum i.e. "Our museum will have no to very less facts and figures."



    Pastiche Scenario

    In pastiche scenario design concept is explored through eyes of one fictional but well defined character. I used Sheldon and Penny from 'The Big Bang Theory' to analyze the current design. This lead to two important insights- 1) the group element was missing in our design and 2) we didnt talk about begginging and ending of the experience.


    Critical Design

    We were already set for the design but we applied concept of critical design to see what unique and powerful elements can be generated for our design. Critical design took us to a different level, we generated concept of extreme embodiment. We as human being can only “see” - “virtually” - one motion at a time. It may be the motion of planet itself or the motion of one or more planets orbiting the sun or the motion of solar system or the galaxy etc.
    But what if we were a planet? A planet spinning itself and orbiting at the same time around the Sun at high speed? A planet goes through all kinds environmental changes. What if those were experienced by us by becoming a planet?


    Experience Prototype

    Our initial concept asked people to create their own planet/ solar system so that they develop better understanding of the planets. Our critical design lead us to a totally different direction of embodied experience of a planet i.e. how does it feel to be a planet? Merging them lead to concept that users can create their own planet and then experience that planet by embodying them. To achieve this we thought of syncing virtual (or mixed) reality movement with the real world movement (or ride). We wanted to test out the experience that it will generate for this we created the experience prototype testing the experience.

    The user is first asked to wear a VR headset then he is taken through 2 different journeys- 1) he is pushed around in linear motion the video is not synced properly to the movement. 2) he is pushed in circular motion and the video also moves in similar speed. We wanted to see how users perceive the experience.


    Final Design

    Pass through the narrow and dark aisle from the entrance, visitors will enter the main hall of the exhibit covered by 360 degree screenage of the real space and a huge dome, which shows the projection of the moving planets created by visitors. With the help of hologram and magnetic levitation technology, visitors can make their own planets by choosing the size, surface and distance from the fixed star (the “sun”) through gestures. Once a group of people complete creating their planets, the system will lock in and adjust the distances of each station, then the individual station will start to spinning and rotating at the calculated speeds. Visitors are able to see the solar system which they create together through holograms, feel the movement of the planet and experience being a planet. When the movement is finished, the solar system created by visitors will be projected on the dome screen, thus people can lay down on the floor to watch the amazing galaxy created by different visitors. For safety’s sake, children and the elderly can only embody planets closer to the edge, as they rotate more slowly.
