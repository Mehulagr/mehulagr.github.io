Skweezee

Soft Objects that Sense their Shape Shifting

Project Guide:
Prof. Luc Geurts and Prof. Vero Vanden Abeele, GROUPT - Leuven Engineering College, Belgium

Project Duration:
May '13 - July '13

Blog:
skweezee.wordpress.com

Skweezees are soft objects, filled with conductive padding that can be deformed or squeezed by applying pressure. The electrodes sense the shape shifting of the conductive filling by measuring the changing resistance between every possible pair of electrodes. Moreover, Skweezees can be programmed to recognize specific shape deformations; users can record their own squeeze gestures and these gestures are afterwards distinguished. Problem statement was to convert the Skweezee system to a usable product.

Literature and similar products were studied to identify the various fields were this product would be beneficial. Based on this list we converged onto various specific problem areas based on various criteria like technological effectiveness, time frame etc. Remote cushion was chosen as context of use for prototyping. The users were given cushion and asked to perform various gestures related to functions of the remote. All participants will first perform the gestures blindfolded or normally. Then they were asked to modify cushion such that it helps them assist in performing gestures. Classification of gestures was done, followed by analysis to find the gestures appropriate for users. Prototype was developed based on the gestures which were defined using user research.
