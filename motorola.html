    Designing for first responders
    Motorola Solutions Internship


    Project Duration: May'16 - Aug'16 | Team: 3 | As a part of internship at Motorola Solutions


    Summary: Motorola Solutions specializes in public safety products like radio, wearable cameras, etc. Last summer, I was an UX Design and Research intern at 'Innovation and Design Lab' of Motorola Solutions where one of the project was to study the impact of self-driving cars on policing and other parallel project was to design a text based input system for facilitating informal communication between police officers.

    My tasks: Contextual Inquiry, Qualitative Research, Literature Research, User Journey, Conceptualization, Usability Testing


    PROBLEM STATEMENTS

    Policing with self driving cars

    How can life of patrol officer and policing in general change with self-driving cars?

    Input Method for Patrol officers

    How can we design an input system for informal text based communication between officers?


    These projects are under NDA so I can't share the entire details but here is a glipmse of what I did during internship.



    SECONDARY RESEARCH

    Self driving cars

    Self driving cars market is changing rapidly. Many tech companies and automobile companies have released their vision of self-driving cars. We tried to compare these visions to identify comonalities and interesting elements among these visions.


    Input method

    Primary reason for designing a new input method was that the device the officers were using for input has considerably smaller screen size and the officers have to always maintain their situational awareness. The error rate was really high when we tried typing on these devices using traditional mobile keyboard, so we looked at other apps and found that it didn't make a lot of difference on the error rate.



    OBSERVATION AND CONTEXTUAL INQUIRY

    Day in a life of a patrol officer

    As a part of user research, I went on a ride along with a patrol officer. During this ride along I was looking for her pattern of using her vehicle, her communication habbits and her day-to-day activities (How does she start her day? What happens once she recieves call from dispatch?).





    USER JOURNEY & AEIOU MAPPING

    What do they need in moments that matter?

    Analyzing primary and secondary research data, we grouped officer activities/ tasks in 5 key touchpoints start of shift, proactive patrol etc. We further expanded these touchpoints using AEIOU mapping into user needs, tools, officer activities/ tasks and information flow.





    IDEATION

    Officer-Vehicle patnership

    We went through multiple brainstorming, design critique and iteration sessions to come up designs that aim to support the police officers in every task they do.


    How do you make the texting smarter?

    Looking at information flow, we wondered if there was a smarter/ contextual way to send some information? More details about this can be discussed over call.




    TESTING

    Can we actually improve efficiency of their texting using our design?

    We tested proof of concept version of our input method/ mobile keyboard with two other input method using error rate, usability score (SUS), task load (NASA TLX) and qualitative feedback. Even though it was a proof of concept we saw an improvement in efficiency when the officers used our input method. The testing revealed limitation of our proof of concept and helped us in understanding if the existing direction can actually help us in improving efficiency of the officers and would they be able to adapt to a different input method?
