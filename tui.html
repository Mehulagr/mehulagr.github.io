    Tangible User Interfaces

    Evaluating Shape-Change as a Feedback Modality for Tangible User Interface

    Brief

    We have come a long way from command line interfaces to madern tablet based interface. Even Tangible User Interface have started increasing. But feedback and output in these interfaces is still limited to screen based visual modality and audio modality. Here we have prototypedand compared two interface, one with the conventional visual feedback using LED and other with the shape changing based feedback.

    Introduction

    This was an open-ended project where the aim was to understand the field of TUI, find a gap in it and then provide suggestion to fill the gap based on experimentation.

    It involved two parts of literature study. In first part of literature study, we went over through theoretical paper of TUI i.e. these papers helped in establishing firm basic understanding of TUI and helped us in developing mechanism to analyse TUI products.


    Analysis

    Forty TUI products were analysed based on the understanding developed from the theoretical foundation papers in TUI. Although the theoretical foundation mention that feedback modality pays an important part, yet most of TUI projects simply used visual (screen based) or audio modality for providing feedback.

    So, we decided to take a different route and provide feedback by using shape changing interface. Thus, aim of the experiment was to “evaluate user’s satisfaction and system’s usability for tangible system with shape-changing modality over non-screen based visual (illumination based) modality.”


    Prototyping

    Two systems were conceptualized one with shape changing feedback and other with convention visual feedback (using LEDs).

    We prototyped mechanisms using legos, paper and anything we could lay on our hands on. Then we decided on mechanisms based on their feasability.

    Prototyping
    Final Mechanisms

    The two conceptual models were converted into the testable prototypes.


    Experimentation

    The experiment was within-subject design with 10 participants using both the systems. The users evaluated both the systems on SUS scale and Attrakdiff questionnaire. They were asked to give out their preference for each system. The SUS score for Visual Feedback system is 72 whereas SUS score for Shape Changing System is 66.5.


    Conclusion

    Tangible interfaces couple the physical world with the digital information. Thus, change in material properties become a natural choice of interaction for such interfaces. Shape changing feedback system was found to be attracting attention of the user by the virtue of its movement and so they have potential to be more effective in catch immediate attraction of user particularly over the distance. The results also suggest that shape change as a feedback fared better to convey system status and ordinal information whereas non-screen visual (illumination based) fared better for quantitative information.
